How they work

How do they work

E-bikes typically consist of a battery, motor, extra components, and a reinforced frame. The battery powers the electric motor, which assists the rider’s pedaling. The motor can be engaged and disengaged using a throttle or pedal-assist system. Modern geometry and engineering help e-bikes handle well despite their weight, which is typically around 20 pounds heavier than standard bikes.

Electric bikes

Electric bikes

An electric bicycle, also known as an e-bike, is a motorized bicycle with an integrated electric motor used to assist propulsion. E-bikes are designed to provide a boost to the rider’s pedaling, making it easier to ride longer distances and tackle hilly terrain. They are available in various categories, including pedelecs, S-Pedelecs, and power-on-demand e-bikes.
